Train Accessibility

A Visual Analysis Of Victoria's Train Network.

Importance of Trains

From census data spanning 2011 to 2021, trains are the most used choice of public transport for commuters (census results for 2026 year are not released at the time of this visualization).
In Victoria, train commuters in both Metropolitan and Regional LGAs are growing.

Railway Structure And Train Services

Train stations are predominately concentrated in Metro LGA regions. In Regional LGAs, train station infrastructure becomes more sparse as we move away from Melbourne. Some Regional LGAs don't have access to any train station infrastructure in their respective region.
There exists an imbalance in the train services relative to the population size of a particular LGA. Normalized per 10,000 residents, there is a significant disparity between Regional and Metro LGAs, even when looking at more popular and major regional hubs such as Ballarat.
Rail accessibility is skewed across Victoria, with metro LGAs typically having higher services per 10,000 residents when compared to Regional LGAs.

Population Impact

This demonstrates that there is a lack of relationship between population and services. even when Regional LGAs have a greater population, smaller Metropolitan LGAs still have more frequent services. This suggests that Regional LGA train infrastructure are not optimized to handle the growing population. For instance, Greater Geelong has a large population, but are not supported by more frequent services.

Wait Time Analysis

For both weekends and weekdays, average wait times for Regional train services are higher than wait times for Metro train services, demonstrating the accessibility imbalance between these LGAs in Victoria.

Availability: Services And Location

Metropolitan LGAs have significantly more services per hour when compared to Regional LGAs throughout all periods of the day.
Metro LGAs have significantly more train stations compared to Regional. This demonstrates that Regional LGAs have lower accessibility to train stations, and therefore trains.
